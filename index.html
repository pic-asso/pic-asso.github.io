About Me

Have you heard of Ikigai? It's a Japanese concept referring to one's "reason for being" — the intersection of what you love, what you're good at, what the world needs, and what you can be paid for. Finding it is said to bring purpose, fulfilment, and even longevity.

Now in my thirties and after five years in academia pursuing my PhD, I'm still on that journey. But experience has given me some clear signposts:

  • I thrive when surrounded by inspiring people who share a lust for success.
  • I love learning something new every single day — from cooking to Slavic languages.
  • I love teaching everything I know and sharing knowledge with anyone it may serve.
  • And yes — for a reasonable salary!

All of this without losing sight of the most important ingredient for happiness: a healthy balance between personal and professional life.
